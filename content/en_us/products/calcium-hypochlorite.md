Calcium Hypochlorite (Ca(OCl)₂), commonly known as bleaching powder or "Perchloron," is a white crystalline solid with a strong chlorine odor. It is manufactured by reacting chlorine gas with slaked lime (calcium hydroxide). With an available chlorine content of 65–70%, it offers significantly higher disinfecting power per unit weight than liquid sodium hypochlorite, making it cost-effective for large-scale or remote applications.

### Key Applications

*   **Drinking Water Disinfection**: Primary disinfectant for municipal water treatment plants and emergency water purification.
*   **Swimming Pool Sanitation**: Shock treatment and routine maintenance to eliminate bacteria, algae, and pathogens.
*   **Wastewater Treatment**: Disinfection of effluent before discharge to meet environmental standards.
*   **Industrial Bleaching**: Bleaching agent for pulp, paper, textiles, and laundry operations.
*   **Food & Beverage Sanitation**: Surface and equipment disinfection in food processing facilities.
*   **Agriculture**: Disinfection of irrigation water and farm equipment.
*   **Emergency Hygiene**: Rapid deployment disinfectant in disaster relief and field sanitation.

> **Safety Note**: Calcium Hypochlorite is a strong oxidizer and may cause fire or explosion when in contact with organic materials, oils, or reducing agents. Store in a cool, dry, well-ventilated area away from flammable substances and direct sunlight. Full PPE including goggles and chemical-resistant gloves must be worn during handling.
