Sodium Hypochlorite (NaOCl), historically known as Javelle Water (Eau de Javel), is a clear to pale yellow aqueous solution with a characteristic chlorine odor. It is produced by reacting chlorine gas with a cold, dilute sodium hydroxide solution. As the most widely used liquid bleach and disinfectant worldwide, it provides broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity at low concentrations and is the active ingredient in household bleach (3–5%) and industrial-grade products (10–16%).

### Key Applications

*   **Water & Wastewater Treatment**: Continuous dosing for potable water disinfection and effluent treatment in municipal plants.
*   **Industrial Bleaching**: Pulp and paper bleaching, textile whitening, and laundry operations.
*   **Food & Beverage Industry**: Cleaning and sanitizing of process equipment, pipelines, and packaging surfaces (CIP systems).
*   **Healthcare & Hospitals**: Surface disinfection, instrument sterilization support, and biohazard decontamination.
*   **Swimming Pools**: Automated dosing systems for pool sanitation.
*   **Household Bleach**: Diluted formulations for domestic cleaning and stain removal.
*   **Odor Control**: Oxidation of hydrogen sulfide and mercaptans in wastewater and industrial exhaust scrubbers.

> **Safety Note**: Sodium Hypochlorite is a corrosive oxidizer. Contact with acids releases toxic chlorine gas. Avoid mixing with ammonia, acids, or other cleaning agents. Store in UV-protected, sealed HDPE containers at temperatures below 25 °C to minimize degradation. PPE including face shield and chemical-resistant gloves must be worn during handling and transfer.
