Calcium Chloride (CaCl₂) is an inorganic salt available as white hygroscopic flakes, pellets, or a concentrated aqueous solution. It is produced either as a by-product of the Solvay soda ash process or via the direct reaction of calcium carbonate with hydrochloric acid. Its strong exothermic dissolution and high water-absorption capacity make it indispensable across diverse industrial and infrastructure applications.

### Key Applications

*   **De-icing & Anti-icing**: Melts ice and snow at temperatures as low as −52 °C; widely used on roads, runways, and pavements.
*   **Dust Control**: Applied to unpaved roads and mine hauls to suppress dust by absorbing atmospheric moisture.
*   **Oil & Gas Drilling**: Completion and workover fluids; increases brine density without solids.
*   **Concrete Acceleration**: Admixture to accelerate setting time in cold-weather concreting.
*   **Food Preservation**: Firming agent in canned vegetables and cheese production (E509).
*   **Desiccant**: Industrial drying agent for gases and organic solvents.
*   **Refrigeration**: Calcium chloride brine used as a secondary refrigerant in cold storage systems.
*   **Wastewater Treatment**: Precipitation of fluoride and phosphate ions.
