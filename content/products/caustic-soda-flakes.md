Caustic Soda Flakes (Sodium Hydroxide, NaOH) is a white, hygroscopic solid produced primarily by the chlor-alkali electrolysis of sodium chloride brine. The flake form offers easy handling, fast dissolution, and convenient dosing compared to liquid grades, making it one of the most widely traded industrial alkalis in global markets.

### Key Applications

*   **Chemical Manufacturing**: Raw material for producing PVC stabilizers, soaps, detergents, and synthetic fibers (rayon, viscose).
*   **Pulp & Paper**: Kraft pulping process to digest wood chips and recover cooking chemicals.
*   **Alumina Refining**: Bauxite digestion in the Bayer process for aluminum production.
*   **Water Treatment**: pH adjustment and neutralization of acidic wastewater streams.
*   **Textile Industry**: Mercerization of cotton to improve strength, luster, and dye uptake.
*   **Food Industry**: Food-grade processing including olive curing and pretzel glazing (E524).
*   **Petroleum Refining**: Removal of sulfur compounds and acid gas scrubbing.

> **Safety Note**: Caustic Soda Flakes are highly corrosive and exothermic upon dissolution. Appropriate PPE (gloves, goggles, chemical-resistant clothing) must be worn during handling. Store in a dry, cool, well-ventilated area away from moisture and incompatible materials (acids, organic materials).
